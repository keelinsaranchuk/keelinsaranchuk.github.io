AUSTRALIAN WILDFIRES

Where are the fires?

There have been wildfires in every Australian State, but New South Whales has been hit the hardest
Fires have torn through bushland, forest and national parks like the Blue Mountains. Some of Australia's largest cities have also been affected including Melbourne and Sydney
These fires have damaged homes and thick clouds of smoke have blanketed cities. Early December, the smoke was so bad in Sydney that it was declared 11 times the "hazardous level"
The fires, however, do range in size and land coverage. Some small blazes are started and immediately contained but the biggest blazes have been burning for months, occupying entire hectares of land.

What is causing the fires?

Each year, the fire season starts making it easy for fires to start and spread
Natural causes are to blame for the most of the time, like lightning strikes in drought affected forests
Humans can also be to blame. NSW police have charged at least 24 people with deliberately starting bush fires, and have taken legal action against 183 people for fire-related offenses since November

Why are the fires so bad?

Fire season has always been dangerous in Australia. But conditions have been unusually severe this year, fanning the flames and making firefighting conditions particularly difficult.
Australia has been experiencing one of its worst droughts of history. Also, unusually strong winds have caused fire and smoke to spread more rapidly.
Experts say climate change has worsened the scope of and impact of natural disasters like fires and floods. Weather conditions have been growing more extreme and fires in Australia have been starting earlier and earlier in the season

What have been the damages so far?
  • At least 27 million acres have been damaged
  • The fires have killed 29 people
  • The fires have damaged 2,500 homes
  • Around 1.25 billion animals have been lost
  • Some cities have been evacuated, due to hazardous air and climate
  • Scientists also fear long term damage has been made
  • etc
Latest relief in the fires

Recently severe thunderstorms have pelted some regions of Australia suffering from huge wildfires. This rain brought huge relief to firefighters battling some of the worst fires they had ever seen
Rain has fallen in most firegrounds in the Australia, however it wasn't enough to contain the fires. With 30 that are yet to be contained and 82 still burning.
Rain hit most of the major cities with water flowing down the streets of Melbourne and Sydney. Many forecaster have predicted even more rain to come but others fear this will cause flooding.
Unfortunately, in other news, lightning from the storms has sparked new grass fires in New South Whales and Victoria. Updates will come on these fires
